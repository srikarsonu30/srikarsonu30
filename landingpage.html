TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, is a monument steeped in both history and beauty. Built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632, it enshrines the tomb of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Its construction, lasting over two decades, involved an estimated 20,000 artisans and represents a fusion of Islamic, Persian, and Indian architectural styles. Today, the Taj Mahal is not just a stunning tourist destination, but also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized as "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage."
